League Etiquette and Participation Rules

The league only works if everyone helps make it work.

We understand that everyone has jobs, travel, tournaments, injuries, and personal commitments. These rules are not meant to be overly strict. They exist to keep the league fair, organized, competitive, and enjoyable for everyone involved.

RULE 01

Scheduling Comes First

The biggest cause of league disruption is delayed scheduling.

By joining the league, players are expected to:

  • Reply to scheduling chats promptly.
  • Give actual available dates and times.
  • Make a reasonable effort to complete matches before deadlines.
  • Be flexible when possible.

Sending vague replies like "Maybe," "Will check," or "Busy this week" without proper follow-up may be treated as non-participation in scheduling.

Scheduling Expectations

Every unscheduled match affects your teammate, your opponents, court bookings, and the season timeline.

Please avoid:

  • Ignoring scheduling chats.
  • Repeatedly delaying decisions.
  • Waiting until the final days to organize.
  • Making others constantly chase for availability.
  • Last-minute cancellations without good reason.

If you cannot reasonably commit time during the season window, it is better to sit out a season than delay multiple matches.

Match Deadlines

Deadlines exist to keep the season moving.

In general:

  • Matches should be organized early.
  • Waiting until the final few days is discouraged.
  • No extensions are granted for event due dates.

If a match remains unresolved, the committee may issue a forfeit, a no-result, or another ruling based on scheduling effort shown by both sides.

Scheduling effort matters.

Availability Expectations

By joining the league, players are expected to:

  • Be reasonably available during the season.
  • Prioritize league matches within the deadline period.
  • Communicate travel plans or conflicts early.

Heavy travel schedules or prolonged unavailability may affect replacement decisions, match rulings, and future participation priority.

RULE 02

Injuries and Replacements

Injuries happen. The league will always try to handle them reasonably and fairly.

However, replacements are one of the most difficult parts of running a competitive league, especially during playoffs.

Injuries vs Avoidable Unavailability

Injuries are treated differently from travel, poor communication, last-minute withdrawals, and failure to schedule proactively.

The league may offer flexibility for legitimate injuries, but avoidable unavailability may not receive the same accommodation.

Replacement Principles

Replacement rules may vary depending on the stage of the season.

General principles:

  • The only time substitutions are allowed without approval is when the replacement is a direct teammate, the original player is injured and unable to play for the foreseeable future, and the opponents agree to the substitution.
  • In all other cases, replacements require committee approval.
  • Replacements should preserve competitive fairness.
  • Emergency substitutions may require committee approval.
  • Playoffs and Finals may have stricter replacement rules.
  • Out-of-league replacements are forbidden.

The committee reserves the right to reject replacements that create major competitive imbalance.

Repeated Unavailability

Repeated issues involving scheduling, travel conflicts, late withdrawals, and replacement needs may affect future season invitations, priority for returning spots, and eligibility decisions.

The league relies heavily on player reliability.

RULE 03

Respect Everyone's Time

Every player in the league is balancing work, family, training, and other commitments.

Please communicate clearly and respectfully.

A fast response, even if unavailable, is always better than silence.

RULE 04

Competitive Etiquette

The league is competitive, but respect comes first.

Players are expected to:

  • Respect opponents and partners.
  • Respect organizers and committee rulings.
  • Compete honestly and in good spirit.

Trash talk and banter are part of the fun. Personal attacks, toxic behavior, or repeated unsportsmanlike conduct are not.

RULE 05

League First Mindset

The league succeeds because players collectively buy into the structure.

That means:

  • Helping schedule quickly.
  • Being responsive.
  • Making compromises when reasonable.
  • Thinking beyond just your own matches.

The smoother everyone cooperates, the better the experience becomes for the entire league.

RULE 06

Committee Discretion

Not every situation can be perfectly covered by written rules.

The committee reserves the right to make rulings in edge cases involving:

  • Scheduling disputes.
  • Injuries.
  • Replacements.
  • Travel conflicts.
  • Competitive fairness.

The goal of every ruling is to preserve fairness, competitive integrity, match quality, and league continuity.
